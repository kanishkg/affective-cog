Instructions

In this online experiment, you will be presented with short social reasoning scenarios. Each scenario includes a story, a question, and at least two answer options. Your task is to select the best answer based on your understanding of the scenario.

An example scenario can look like this:

Story

Gary is a high school student who is passionate about music and dreams of becoming a professional musician. He is considering whether to join the school band or the school choir to gain more experience and exposure. Gary believes that he can control his chances of being accepted into either group by preparing well for the auditions and showcasing his talent. Gary wants to join the school choir because he believes it will help him improve his vocal skills and provide him with opportunities to perform in various events. He does not want to join the school band because he thinks it may not give him enough time to focus on his individual music practice. Gary auditions for both the school band and the school choir. Gary is accepted into the school band but not the choir.

Question

How does Gary feel?

Answer Options

  1. joyful
  2. frustrated
  3. grateful
  4. disappointed

Instructions

In total, you will answer 42 questions. It is important to read each story carefully and respond to the questions to the best of your ability. Pick the most likely option. Each story is independent, and does not relate to any of the other stories.

Comprehension Quiz

1. Your task in this online experiment is to evaluate the quality of different short social reasoning scenarios based on the understandability of the story and whether the question and answer options make sense with respect to the story.

2. Each short social reasoning scenario consists of a story, a question, and different answer options.

3. There are at least two answer options.

4. The study focuses on testing someone's social reasoning abilities using short mathematical reasoning scenarios.

5. All scenarios in this study involve a short story followed by a question with exactly three multiple-choice response options for you to select from.
